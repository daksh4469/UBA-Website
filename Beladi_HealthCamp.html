    Beladi

    • Date: September 26, 2017

    • Village: Beladi

    • Health Checkups Done: 161

    Under the health initiative in Beladi-Salhapur village, during a General Health and Women Health and Hygiene Survey, it was observed that most of the village people were suffering from the common illnesses and allergic problem. It was also observed that the village lacks the presence of a female doctor due to which many problems of the female populace are ignored. Hence most of the people consult the doctors of nearby cities, which can be dangerous in case of undiagnosed disease and emergencies.

    Therefore, it is the necessary to avail better health facilities to all the village people through a free Health Camp. It will also create awareness towards the health responsibilities in the villagers and will be very helpful to the families residing below the poverty line.

    The major problem is the lack of awareness among the people. Through the Health Survey and interaction with the people of the village over such a long period, the team realized that many people of the village are unaware about the various health policies offered by the government.

    Also, some people are afraid to go any other places for treatment. Women used to involve in household activities as well as in the work they do. So, the complaint of back-paining, joints pain etc. are generally neglected.

    Therefore, the purpose of the event is to treat the village people and aware them about the policies and the facilities that government offers to the citizens.

    The Health Camp conducted by UBA in Beladi-Salhapur have greatly benefitted the poor section of the society as well as all the age groups ranging from less than a year to more than 85. Through these camps, the villagers are given prescription, basic health problems and sicknesses are diagnosed with free medicines. The camp was conducted in Primary School due to its large space and prime location.

    Both males and females have come out in fair proportion in the camp. A good figure is achieved i.e., treating 161 patients in total, which means the camp had benefitted many people.
