Rural Transformation Through Digitilization-An approach

Highlights of the lecture
  • Mr Vijay Pratap enriched the audience with his 17-year long journey with working in rural India. It was his determination towards transforming it using Information Technology, that had led him to where he is.
  • Mr Vijay Pratap briefed about his start-up- EkGaon, whose services have enriched lives of over a million rural households in India alone over the last thirteen years of efforts.
  • EkGaon is a unique 'For Profit' social enterprise focused on providing utility services for farmers, rural businesses & under-served women; along with the large urban migrant labour population of aspiring consumers.
  • It is the first company to provide mobile phone enabled financial services delivery platform in South Asia, with its market connect platform, ekgaon.com provides high quality 'Direct from Farm Produce' that is healthy, organic certified and naturally grown from farms of small farmers to urban customers.
  • The company has its headquarters in New Delhi with its regional office in Madurai (Tamil Nadu) and Mandla (Madhya Pradesh) in India.
  • Mr Vijay Pratap briefly spoke about the plentiful civilizations the country has had, and how India is still inferior to the developed countries.
  • He briefed about the principles of M.K.Gandhi, J.C.Kumarappa, J.P.Narayan and Vinoba Bhave, which had been the major drivers of his work.
  • The work of Dr Verghese Kurien, ‘Father of The White Revolution’ and ‘The Man behind Amul’, had been his deepest motivators, with Amul being one of the billion-dollar food companies in India.
  • He addressed the problems behind the food companies’ decreasing growth rate and the major problems farmers face in India. A reduction by 30% in the R&D budget in agriculture was a striking piece for the audience to hear.

The event was highly appreciated by the audience and received warm feedback. The audience actively interacted with Dr Anil Swarup during the event and it was a huge success.
