    Beladi

    • Date: September 29, 2019

    • Village: Beladi

    • Houses Surveyed: 75

    • Cloth bags distributed: 120

    Working for Swatchta Hi Seva on 29th September 2019, Unnat Bharat Abhiyan IIT Roorkee took a step forward towards a greener and cleaner future by organizing a Plastic Free India Campaign. Plastic Free India is a campaign, which comes under Swachh Bharat mission governed by the Prime Minister of India, Shri Narendra Modi, launched on 2 nd October 2014. The mission aims to achieve Swachh Bharat by 2019, as a fitting tribute to Mahatma Gandhi on his 150 th birth anniversary.

    Plastic pollution has become a major problem in these days, as per Ministry of Environment, India generates 16000 ton/day out of which only 9000 ton/day being recycled and the remaining pollutes the soil, clogs drains, pollutes water and kill the animals with its indigestive nature. Also, the improper handling of plastic waste in open spaces and road margins give rise to disease in humans as well as not good for earth.

    Team under UBA IITR visited the adopted village Beladi-Salhapur for a no- plastic drive in the village.

    The teams went door-to-door, collecting single-use plastic bags and distributing cloth bags in their place. Villagers were explained the adverse effects of using plastic on the environment and water bodies. Also, village and household surveys were done in each of the villages to understand ground level problems for future initiatives better.

    A total of 75 households in all were surveyed, and 120 cloth bags were distributed.
