About Winter School 2020
Unnat Abhiyan Bharat, IIT Roorkee is organizing Winter school 2020 a three-day event scheduled from January 17, 2020, to January 19, 2020. It offers an unmatchable learning experience with some renowned Professors and experts to the participants in the remarkable fields of Social Entrepreneurship, Accessible India, Issues of Indian Economy, Personal & Interpersonal development. It consists of Guest Lectures, Workshops, Field visits, Panel discussion, Group Discussion and many more.

Day 1

Winter School 2020 was organized by Team UBA, IIT Roorkee from January 17 to January 19.about-uba.about-uba. More than 30 participants from different parts of the country witnessed and participated in a plethora of activities organized as part of Winter School 2020. It was kickstarted by Rajiv Dhar sir and Aashish Pandey sir, who was the Chief Guest in the opening ceremony. This was followed by a Model United Nations (MUN), chaired by Mr. Sankalp Chawla and Mr. Sahil Sihag, where participants showcased their skills of debating and diplomacy.

Day 2

Day two witnessed a number of informative and exciting guest lectures, starting from Vijay Pratap Singh Aditya on the 'Growing Roots of India'. Following this was an interactive session on 'Networking' conducted by Professor Vinay K Nangia. The highlight of Winter School 2020 was the guest lecture delivered by Mr. Anil Swarup, former Education and Coal Secretary of India, on 'Making Things Happen in the Government', which was widely appreciated by everyone.img-responsive. The proceedings of the day were concluded by a brainstorming session of 'Throne of Dilemma'.

Day 3

The proceedings of Day three were initiated by a field visit to village Meerpur, which provided participants an insight into village life. Mr. Abhishek Saini then guided the budding minds in the field of 'Social Entrepreneurship' by his inspiring guest lecture on the same. Winter School 2020 ended on a high by a 'Networking Workshop', in which participants indulged in a number of fun activities like Personal Judgement and Napoleon.
